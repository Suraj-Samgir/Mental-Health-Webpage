Self Assesment:

Mental Health America (MHA) Screening

This online screening tool is one of the quickest and easiest ways to determine whether you are experiencing symptoms of a mental health condition. It includes tests for depression, anxiety, ADHD, bipolar disorder, psychosis & schizophrenia, PTSD, eating disorders, addiction, and more.

Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory (MMPI-2)

First published in 1989, the MMPI-2 is the world’s most widely used psychometric test for measuring mental health ailments that feature as forms of psychopathology.

Beyond Blue

They offer simple self-assessment tools to check your mental health. The K10 or Mental Health Check-in can provide resources and support.
PsyCom4: They offer free, medically reviewed quizzes for a proactive approach to your mental health and well-being.

Healthy Place

They provide various mental health assessment tools including observation, interviews, checklists, rating scales, and questionnaires.

Therapy Options:

Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

This is an evidence-based treatment that examines the link between thoughts, emotions, and behavior. In CBT sessions, clients learn how to recognize cognitive distortions or “thinking traps,” such as all-or-nothing thinking, catastrophizing, and making faulty assumptions.

Psychoanalysis and Psychodynamic Therapies

These therapies focus on uncovering and examining meaningful events or patterns from the past that may be influencing a person’s current state. Once the negative thought pattern is identified, clients practice more rational and positive ways to view situations and experiences.

Interpersonal Therapy

In this approach, the patient and therapist work together to identify the patient's diagnosis and examine it within the context of their lives and experiences. Strengthening relationships and establishing and deepening support systems are essential in this type of therapy.

Mentalization Therapy

This therapy refers to a person’s capacity to distinguish between their emotions and the feelings of others. This process can also help patients understand how their emotions are connected to their actions.

Behavior Therapy

This therapy focuses on changing unwanted behaviors through reward systems or desensitization techniques.

Humanistic Therapy

This therapy focuses on the individual's unique experience and emphasizes personal growth and fulfillment of potential.

Integrative Therapy

This therapy combines elements from different therapeutic tools and approaches to fit the individual's unique needs.

Complementary and Alternative Therapies

These can include things like yoga, meditation, aromatherapy, hypnotherapy, herbal remedies, and acupuncture.

Self Care:

Self Care Tips

Self-care is the collective set of practices that you engage in to make sure that you’re happy and healthy. It includes practices that make us feel good about ourselves, our futures, and our lives. Examples of self-care include seeing a doctor to determine the underlying cause of headaches, drinking plenty of water, reducing alcohol, caffeine, or sugar intake, signing off work early or skipping social plans to rest, getting enough sleep every night, taking a mental health day when you feel burnt out, not letting your work-life balance get out of control, taking breaks away from the computer to manage eye strain, and prioritizing physical health by scheduling.
